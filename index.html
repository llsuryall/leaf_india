About The Living Earth Foundation

The Living Earth Foundation (LEAF) was founded by Advocate Sreeja Chakraborty with a clear vision: to be a formidable legal voice for environmental protection and justice in India. Sreeja, an accomplished environmental lawyer, established LEAF in Bengaluru to address pressing ecological concerns through strategic litigation.

Our foundation operates at the forefront of environmental law, taking on critical cases in the High Courts and the National Green Tribunal (NGT). We believe in empowering local communities and standing firm against powerful corporations and unsustainable development projects that threaten our natural heritage.

Since its inception, LEAF has provided legal representation in over 20 public interest cases, working tirelessly with civil society groups, environmental activists, affected communities, and concerned citizens across Karnataka, Goa, and Kerala.

Our Impactful Work & Landmark Cases

Hubballi-Ankola Railway Line

LEAF actively opposed the proposed Hubballi-Ankola railway line, which threatened to cause extensive deforestation and irreversible damage to the rich biodiversity of the Western Ghats. Our legal interventions aim to protect this critical ecological hotspot.

Read More

Honnavara Port Expansion

We've taken a stand against the ecologically sensitive Honnavara port expansion project, which poses threats to coastal ecosystems, marine life, and the livelihoods of local fishing communities. Our work includes highlighting human rights violations and environmental damage.

Read More

Sharavati Pumped Storage Project

LEAF has filed multiple representations regarding the Sharavati pumped storage project, particularly concerning its impact on the Eco-Sensitive Zone (ESZ) of the Sharavathi Lion-tailed Macaque Sanctuary and the Jog Falls area. We advocate for sustainable tourism and development.

Read More

Karmal Ghat Highway Expansion

One of LEAF's early successes was halting the indiscriminate felling of 30,000 trees for a highway expansion through Karmal Ghat, Goa, in 2020. We ensured an alternate alignment study and cost-benefit analysis were conducted, protecting this eco-sensitive area.

Read More

Lake Encroachment Case

We assisted communities in a significant case against a real estate company that encroached upon a dry lake bed, blocking 15 rainwater feeder channels. Our involvement led to an inspection by the Karnataka Tank Conservation Authority and helped build a strong legal case.

Read More

Bannerghatta ESZ Litigation

LEAF has filed a Public Interest Litigation (PIL) in the Karnataka High Court concerning the Krishnadoddi Bypass project of KRDCL, which impacts the Eco-Sensitive Zone (ESZ) of Bannerghatta National Park. We strive to protect this vital urban lung.
